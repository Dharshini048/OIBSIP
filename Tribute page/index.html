Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," is a beacon of inspiration for millions. His humility, vision, and dedication to science have made a significant impact on the nation and the world.One of the most inspiring things about Dr. Kalam is his ability to dream big and encourage others to do the same. His famous quote, "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts, and thoughts result in action," has motivated countless individuals to pursue their goals.In addition to his remarkable achievements in aerospace and missile technology, Dr. Kalam was also a passionate advocate for education. He believed that educating the youth was essential for India's progress, and he often engaged with students to ignite their curiosity and passion for learning. His leadership during the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests was a significant milestone, establishing India as a nuclear power.In 2002, he was elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007, during which he was affectionately known as the "People's President" for his approachable nature and commitment to youth empowerment. He inspired countless students through his speeches and writings, including influential books such as "Wings of Fire," his autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," which urged young Indians to harness their potential for national development. Throughout his life, Kalam advocated for a vision of India as a developed nation by 2020, emphasizing the role of education, technology, and innovation. Beyond his scientific and political achievements, Dr. Kalam was a man of deep spirituality and simplicity, living a life dedicated to service and humility. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a profound legacy that continues to inspire generations to dream big and strive for excellence in all endeavors.
